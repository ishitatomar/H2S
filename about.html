FAQ

Who is eligible for financial aid?

Eligibility for financial aid varies depending on the type of aid (scholarships, grants, loans) and the specific program. Generally, eligibility is based on factors such as:

  • Financial need (determined by FAFSA or similar forms)
  • Academic standing
  • Enrollment status (full-time, part-time)
  • Citizenship or eligible non-citizen status
  • Specific program requirements (e.g., major, location)

It's best to check the specific eligibility requirements for each financial aid program you are interested in.

How do I apply for financial aid?

The application process for financial aid typically involves these steps:

  1. Complete the Free Application for Federal Student Aid (FAFSA): This is the most common form for federal and state aid.
  2. Create an account on our platform: If you are using our platform, register and complete your profile.
  3. Gather necessary documents: Collect required documents such as tax returns, W-2 forms, and student identification.
  4. Submit applications: Submit your FAFSA and any other required applications by the deadlines.
  5. Review award letters: Once processed, you'll receive award letters from schools or aid providers detailing your financial aid package.
  6. Accept or decline aid: Decide which aid you want to accept and complete any necessary paperwork.

Always adhere to deadlines and provide accurate information to ensure a smooth application process.
